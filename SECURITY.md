# Security Policy

We take the security of the STATION8 Public Market website seriously and appreciate the work of security researchers in identifying and disclosing vulnerabilities responsibly.

## Reporting a vulnerability

Please report suspected vulnerabilities to **security@[domain]**. *(See `docs/DECISIONS_NEEDED.md` D-1 — real address is pending domain confirmation.)*

When reporting, please include:

- A clear description of the issue.
- Steps to reproduce, or proof-of-concept code.
- The affected URL(s) or component(s).
- The potential impact as you understand it.
- Your name and contact information so we can follow up (anonymous reports are accepted).

If the issue is sensitive, please request a PGP key in your initial email and we will coordinate encrypted follow-up.

## What to expect

- **Acknowledgement:** within 3 business days.
- **Initial assessment:** within 7 business days.
- **Resolution timeline:** depends on severity; we will keep you informed throughout.

## Safe-harbor

We will not pursue legal action against researchers who:

- Make a good-faith effort to avoid privacy violations, destruction of data, and degradation of service.
- Do not exploit a vulnerability beyond the minimum proof-of-concept required to demonstrate it.
- Give us a reasonable opportunity to remediate before public disclosure.
- Do not access, modify, or delete data belonging to other users.

## Out of scope

The following are generally not considered security vulnerabilities for this site:

- Reports based solely on automated scanner output without demonstrated impact.
- Missing security headers on non-sensitive endpoints (we welcome the report; severity will reflect impact).
- Issues requiring physical access to a victim's device.
- Social engineering of staff or customers.
- Denial-of-service via traffic volume.

Thank you for helping keep STATION8 and our visitors safe.
